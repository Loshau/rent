Vendors & Associates

Our vetted network of professionals and skilled trades support every stage of your project—from design and costing to construction and ongoing care.

Professional Services

Architects lead the creative and technical process of turning ideas into buildable spaces. They consult on client needs, analyze sites and codes, develop concept sketches and detailed drawings, coordinate approvals, and collaborate with engineers and contractors. Their work balances aesthetics, functionality, safety, sustainability, and budget to deliver compliant, efficient, and inspiring buildings.

Engineers provide the structural and building services backbone of projects. Structural engineers design safe frameworks and load paths; MEP engineers design mechanical, electrical and plumbing systems for comfort and efficiency. They produce calculations, specifications and drawings, verify code compliance, review shop drawings, visit sites, and solve technical issues that arise during construction and commissioning.

Interior designers plan how spaces feel and function day‑to‑day. They develop layouts, select finishes, lighting, furniture and joinery, and coordinate details that elevate comfort, durability and brand expression. They align aesthetics with user workflows and safety requirements, control budgets through curated options, and supervise installations to achieve a cohesive, high‑quality interior outcome.

Quantity surveyors manage project costs from concept to handover. They prepare cost plans, bills of quantities and tender documents; evaluate bids; administer contracts; track variations; and certify payments. By providing transparent estimates, risk allowances and value engineering options, they protect budgets and help clients make informed decisions throughout the build lifecycle.

Construction & Skilled Trades

Contractors organize labor, materials, equipment and sequencing to build safely and on schedule. They coordinate subcontractors, manage procurement and logistics, implement quality and safety plans, and communicate with consultants and clients. Their site leadership converts drawings into reality while controlling risks, costs and time across every construction phase.

Electricians install and maintain power distribution, lighting, controls and protective devices. They pull cables, terminate panels, fit fixtures, test for continuity and insulation, and commission systems to standards. Their work ensures safety, reliability and energy efficiency for occupants and critical building systems.

Plumbers install and service water supply, drainage and sanitary systems. They route piping, fit valves and fixtures, test pressures and flows, and resolve leaks or blockages. Proper plumbing protects health, conserves water and supports dependable kitchens, bathrooms and building services throughout the property.

Carpenters construct and finish wood elements such as framing, doors, ceilings, cabinets and custom joinery. They read drawings, cut and assemble components, and install hardware and trims. Their craftsmanship improves structural integrity, usability and the overall look and feel of interiors and exteriors.

Painters prepare surfaces, repair defects, apply primers and durable finishes, and deliver consistent color and texture. Through proper surface conditioning and product selection, they protect substrates from weathering and wear, improve hygiene and light reflectance, and elevate the visual character of spaces inside and out.

Property Care & Maintenance

Landscapers design and maintain outdoor environments that are functional, safe and beautiful. They plan softscape and hardscape features, install lawns, plants and irrigation, and provide ongoing pruning, weeding and soil care. Quality landscaping improves curb appeal, supports biodiversity, manages stormwater and creates welcoming communal and private outdoor spaces.

Fumigation specialists assess infestation risks and apply targeted treatments to eliminate pests while protecting occupant health and assets. They select safe chemicals or non‑chemical methods, schedule works to minimize disruption, and advise on preventive measures. Proper pest management safeguards hygiene, structures, equipment and tenant comfort.

Exterminators identify pest species, locate nests and access points, and implement eradication and exclusion strategies. They monitor results, rotate treatments to avoid resistance, and educate occupants on sanitation and storage practices. Effective extermination restores healthy conditions and prevents costly damage to finishes, wiring and stored goods.
